Anaizat Hereim, MBA, PMP

Clearned for Landing: Transitioning to Suit from Salute the Anaizat Way

The decision to transition out of the military is like preparing for a mission you’ve never flown before. You can study the charts, check the weather, and run through every preflight checklist, but nothing prepares you for the moment you actually take off into the unknown. That’s where Anaizat came in. I didn’t start this journey looking for a coach—I was just looking for answers. After years in the Air Force, I had a solid career, a clear path forward, and a strong network. But something kept pulling me in a different direction. I knew I wanted a change, but the how and when weren’t clear. The more I dug into transition resources, the more overwhelming it all became. Enter Anaizat. A former Navy officer, she had navigated this path before and knew exactly what I was up against. From our first conversation, it was clear she wasn’t just another transition coach—she was someone who understood both the practical and emotional weight of this decision. More importantly, she knew how to turn uncertainty into action. Anaizat didn’t just hand me a list of checkboxes. She helped me reshape my mindset. She challenged me to define what I really wanted—not just in a job, but in a career, a lifestyle, and a purpose beyond the uniform. She helped me untangle the mental knots that kept me second-guessing my decision, pushing me to own my transition instead of letting it happen to me. Together, we tackled some of the biggest challenges veterans face: ✔ Translating military experience into civilian terms – She helped me articulate my skills in a way that recruiters and hiring managers understood. ✔ Networking with intention – Instead of just connecting with people, she taught me how to build meaningful relationships that opened doors. ✔ Job search strategy – She guided me through targeting roles that aligned with my skills and ambitions, rather than just applying blindly. ✔ Confidence in a new identity – Perhaps the biggest shift was internal—she helped me see myself not just as an Airman leaving the service, but as a highly capable professional stepping into a new mission. What started as career coaching turned into true mentorship. Anaizat saw potential in me that I wasn’t fully embracing, and she refused to let me settle. She gave me the tools, the perspective, and sometimes the tough love I needed to push forward. Thanks to her guidance, I didn’t just land a role—I launched into a career as a Systems Engineer in the Defense Aerospace sector. Looking back, I realize I wasn’t just transitioning from the military. I was stepping into the next chapter of who I wanted to be. And through it all, Anaizat was there—not leading the way, but making sure I had everything I needed to fly confidently on my own.
