Welcome to a page that chronicles the most thrilling (and occasionally terrifying) experience of my life: transitioning out of the military. It’s a ride filled with reflection, preparation, and the occasional "what am I doing?" moment.

I created this section to help fellow veterans navigate their own transition with fewer head-scratching moments and more clarity.

Spoiler alert: the world outside doesn’t run on chow hall schedules or PT formations.

So, strap in and enjoy the breakdown of how I’m making this shift—complete with resources, real talk, and some tips I wish someone had tattooed on my forehead earlier.

WHY? OPERATION DD-214...

Let’s be honest—transitioning is overwhelming.

Sure, we’ve faced pressure before (deployments, promotions, etc), but entering civilian life comes with a different flavor of anxiety: building resumes, networking, interviews, etc.

Instead, you’re left navigating an endless sea of LinkedIn posts about networking, certifications, and “following your passion.”

This page is my way of showing that you don’t have to do it alone.

It’s my AAR (After Action Report)—but without the jargon (okay, maybe a little).

Services

These are the key services that helped me stay sane (and somewhat confident) during my transition. Think of them as my trusted team—each one played a specific role in helping me move from military life to the civilian world with a bit more ease.

  • American Corporate Partners (ACP) is a nationwide nonprofit dedicated to helping military service members and veterans successfully transition into civilian careers through personalized, one-on-one mentorship. ACP connects veterans with experienced professionals from Fortune 500 companies, top organizations, and various industries to provide career guidance, resume building, networking advice, and job search strategies.
    • What sets ACP apart:
    • Free, Customized Mentorship: Veterans are paired with mentors tailored to their career goals, creating personalized guidance over a 12-month period.
    • Industry Expertise: Mentors come from diverse fields, including IT, engineering, finance, and project management, offering insider knowledge.
    • Proven Success: Many veterans attribute job offers and promotions to the connections and advice gained through ACP.

  • FourBlock is a nonprofit organization designed to bridge the gap between returning veterans and successful civilian careers through a unique, hands-on approach. Its programs combine classroom instruction, mentoring, and networking events to help veterans gain valuable career insights and build long-term professional networks.
    • What sets FourBlock apart:
    • Structured, Semester-Based Program: Designed to build career readiness by guiding veterans through resume building, networking, and job preparation.
    • Partnerships with Top Employers: Veterans gain access to professionals from leading organizations who provide real-world advice and connections.
    • Career Exploration and Development: Offers workshops and events that help veterans explore various industries while developing the necessary professional skills.
